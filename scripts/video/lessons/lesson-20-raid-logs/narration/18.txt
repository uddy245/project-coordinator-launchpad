Projects that don't do this end up with RAID logs full of "risks" that are really issues the team is pretending haven't happened yet. The log gets read as theater rather than as a working tool. Everyone stops believing it. And the one time the risk entry was actually a live risk, nobody notices, because they've trained themselves to skim past them. Keep risks as risks. Convert them to issues when they materialize. Close what's genuinely closed. Be specific about dates and transitions.
